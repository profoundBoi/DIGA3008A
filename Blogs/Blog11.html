Blog 14: Justice and ethics

Justice and ethics

By approaching justice and ethics both as an artist and as a programmer would require me to be aware of how my design affect people’s live and if it does this in a good or in a bad way. I would have to be very mindful and create design that are fair to everyone instead of trying to just avoid offending people. The very beginning of any design should start from an ethical point of view.

In my designs it is important create with responsibility to people and to have a community led practice as stated by Sasha Costanza-Chock (2020) This means that the only way to create designs which are fair and respectful is to have the community involved in the development process because these designs are going to affect the community. We should not be trying to solve peoples’ problems but rather work with people to create the best solution. As designer we do not know everyone’s struggle, but it does not mean that we can’t work with people to find out what these struggles are. In terms of programming, I would need to work with a community to create games and websites that are accessible to them.

One way that we can do this is to resist biases that have often been assumed by previous developers which harmed communities. The way we can avoid these biases is by including people oversight in our projects to get more honest and ethical reviews and feedback. This prevents us as developers and artists from assuming what people want and need. This also build a more transparent system because now people know exactly what they are getting. We can also include more transparency by having our design and systems operate in a transparent way instead of trying to mislead the users.
